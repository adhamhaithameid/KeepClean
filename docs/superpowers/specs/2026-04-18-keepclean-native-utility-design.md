# KeepClean Native Utility Refresh

## Goal

Replace the current over-designed presentation with a calmer macOS utility interface that feels believable, practical, and human-made.

## Direction

- Favor a restrained utility look over a brand-heavy product look.
- Keep the app bright and polished, but use much subtler color and depth.
- Make the `Clean` tab read like a safety-focused control panel instead of a marketing hero.
- Make `Settings` feel closer to a preferences screen.
- Make `About` simple and friendly, not promotional.

## Logo

- Replace the glossy decorative logo with a simpler rounded-square utility mark.
- Use a minimal keyboard/keycap motif with one small cleaning accent.
- Keep the icon recognizable at small sizes and believable as a Mac app icon.

## UI Rules

- Use one primary accent blue and reserve orange only for the timed clean action.
- Reduce gradients, glows, oversized badges, and large headline blocks.
- Use quieter grouped panels, lighter borders, and tighter spacing.
- Keep the two cleaning buttons prominent, but make them feel like utility controls rather than splashy CTA blocks.

## Constraints

- Do not change the lock logic or safety behavior.
- Preserve the existing links, settings, and test identifiers.
- Re-run logic checks, app build verification, and a manual UI pass after the redesign.
